Workflow Automation with Zapier

Context:

Many colleagues outside of engineering were not familiar with automation tools, even though their daily work involved repetitive, manual processes. To empower them, I organized a knowledge-sharing session focused on practical automation.

Audience:

Non-developers from different teams looking to simplify tasks and optimize results.

Content:

  • Gave a conference-style presentation introducing Zapier as a no-code automation platform.
  • Showed real-world integration examples, such as connecting productivity tools, notifications, and reporting workflows.
  • Demonstrated how automation can reduce manual effort, save time, and improve accuracy in daily tasks.

Impact:

  • Helped non-technical colleagues understand the potential of automation.
  • Enabled them to build simple workflows on their own, reducing dependency on developers.
  • Improved team efficiency and inspired the adoption of no-code tools across the company.

Vector Stores in Practice – From Self-Hosted Models to Frontier Models

Context:

With the growing adoption of Large Language Models (LLMs), the challenge of managing private knowledge and grounding responses became central. Many teams were curious about how vector databases work in practice and how they can support AI solutions.

Audience:

Mixed group of technical and non-technical colleagues interested in understanding how AI can securely use enterprise knowledge.

Content:

  • Delivered a session on vector stores and their role in enhancing LLMs with retrieval-augmented generation (RAG).
  • Presented examples using both self-hosted models (Hugging Face embeddings + local vector DBs via Ollama interface) and frontier models (OpenAI with managed vector stores).
  • Showed practical demos of querying enterprise data with semantic search and grounding AI responses in internal knowledge.
  • Compared trade-offs: scalability, cost, security, and ease of integration between self-hosted and frontier setups.

Impact:

  • Increased awareness among colleagues of how vector stores enable private, trustworthy AI assistants.
  • Provided decision-making guidance on when to choose self-hosted vs. managed solutions.
  • Strengthened the team's ability to leverage AI for knowledge retrieval and productivity optimization.
